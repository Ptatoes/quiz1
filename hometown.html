I migrated from Pulau Pinang to Kemaman, Terengganu, at the age of 4 because my parents, both teachers, wanted to be closer to my grandmother's house to help care for her. Kemaman is a charming district located along the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia, celebrated for its stunning beaches, vibrant fishing culture, and delectable local cuisine, particularly its seafood. The area is known for its warm, tropical climate, making it an attractive destination for both locals and tourists. This move brought me great happiness, as Kemaman offers a serene environment with minimal traffic and congestion, allowing me to enjoy a peaceful childhood.

Terengganu, the state where Kemaman is situated, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking landscapes, which include lush rainforests, rolling hills, and pristine coastlines. The state is famous for its traditional crafts, such as batik and songket, as well as its historic sites, including the Terengganu State Museum, one of the largest museums in Southeast Asia. Terengganu is also home to several beautiful islands, such as Redang and Perhentian, known for their crystal-clear waters and vibrant coral reefs, making them popular spots for snorkeling and diving. This peaceful environment has fostered a deep appreciation for nature and a sense of tranquility that continues to influence my life, shaping my values and connection to the world around me.
