Local Food in Terengganu

In Terengganu, a state celebrated for its coastal charm, many local dishes prominently feature fish, making seafood an integral part of the region's culinary identity and cultural heritage. Among the dishes I particularly savor are ketupat sotong, keropok lekor, and sotong celup tepung. Each of these delectable offerings not only highlights the rich flavors of the sea but also exemplifies the creativity and tradition embedded in Terengganuâ€™s vibrant cuisine.

Keropok lekor

A traditional Malaysian fish cracker from Terengganu, made from a mix of fish and sago flour, deep-fried for a crispy texture. It's often served with a spicy dipping sauce and is a popular snack enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Sotong celup tepung

A popular Malaysian street food dish of deep-fried squid coated in a light batter, often served with a sweet and spicy sauce.

Mee Laksam

A spicy noodle dish from the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia, made with thick rice noodles, served in a rich and flavorful broth, and often topped with fish cakes, shrimp, and vegetables.

Nasi Dagang

A fragrant rice dish made with a mixture of white and glutinous rice, served with fish curry (usually tuna), pickled vegetables, and hard-boiled eggs.

Sata

Grilled fish cakes made from fish, coconut, and spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and grilled over charcoal.

Ketupat Sotong

A dish of squid stuffed with glutinous rice, cooked in a sweet and savory coconut milk sauce.
